What is Predictive Maintenance?â€‹

Predictive maintenance is a proactive maintenance strategy that employs data and analytics to predict when an equipment is likely to fail, allowing repairs or replacements to be scheduled just before the failure happens.

This approach contrasts with traditional reactive maintenance, where equipment is serviced only after it breaks down, and preventive maintenance, which involves scheduled maintenance tasks regardless of the equipment's actual condition.

Source: Asset Infinity

Why is it important?â€‹

Predictive maintenance is key strategy employed by industries to optimize the performance of their assets and machinery. Here are some reasons why predictive maintenance is crucial to the industries:

  1. Cost Savings

    Predictive maintenance allows organization to address potential issues before they lead to equipment failure, allowing for optimal scheduling of maintenance during planned downtime, thereby reducing downtime cost.

  2. Optimizes Equipment Utilization

    By addressing problems proactively during planned maintenance windows, organization can ensure that equipment remains operational for more extended periods. Additionally, with continuous monitoring of equipment health through sensors, organizations can also ensure that assets are operating at their optimal performance levels, maximizing utilization.

  3. Extends Equipment Lifespan

    Instead of following a fixed schedule, predictive maintenance relies on the actual condition of equipment to determine when a maintenance is necessary. This condition-based approach, optimizes maintenance intervals, and preserves critical components, therefore ensures the overall longevity and reliability of the entire system.
